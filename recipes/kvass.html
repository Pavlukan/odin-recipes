Kvass from Eastern Europe

Description

There are a lot of different types of kvass recipes, including beet kvass. However, traditional bread kvass is a beer-like beverage made from toasted bread.

It has a long history of being brewed in Eastern Europe. And simple to make at home and doesn’t require any special ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 450 grammes of bread
  • 3 litres of water
  • 2/3 cup of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of bread yeast
  • Flavours such as lemon peel, mint, berries, or raisins (optional)

Steps

  1. Cut the bread into crouton-sized cubes. Toast them in the oven at 180 °C for 20 min.
  2. Toss halfway through to ensure even browning. The goal is to caramelize the sugars, so toast as long as necessary.
  3. Place the toasted bread cubes in a large bowl. Bring the water to a boil, and pour it over the bread.
  4. Let the bread soak for at least 4 hours (up to 24 hours). The goal is to extract the malted sugars into the boiling water.
  5. Strain liquid into a fermentation container. I recommend doing this by pouring the liquid through a cheesecloth-lined strainer, then wringing the wet bread to get all the liquid out.
  6. Stir the sugar and yeast into the liquid. Add any additional flavors at this point.
  7. Leave the kvass to ferment in a warm location to ferment for around 1 week. It will start to foam and bubble significantly after 6-12 hours.
  8. After one week, strain the kvass into swing-top beer bottles and store them in the fridge.
  9. The kvass will continue to ferment and will build up pressure, so use bottles that can handle the build-up of pressure, and pop the lid every few days.
  10. It will be ready to drink after 3 days in the fridge. However, it will continue to ferment for another 1-2 weeks until all the sugars have been consumed by the yeast.
